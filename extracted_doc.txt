Student  Saad  Wael Alrabadi Student Mohammad Safwan Frehat Student Mohammad Fathi Alghazo Student  Abedalqader   Alfaqeh Abstract This graduation project presents  Kawadir , a full-stack Human Resource Management System (HRMS) designed to digitize and streamline core HR operations within organizations. The system was developed using  ASP.NET Core 8  with a Clean Architecture pattern on the backend, backed by a  PostgreSQL  database managed through Entity Framework Core, and an  Angular 19  single-page application on the frontend. The platform supports three user roles — Admin, HR, and Employee — each with tailored access controls enforced via  JWT-based authentication  and role-based authorization. Core modules include employee lifecycle management, department and position structuring, attendance tracking with clock-in/clock-out functionality, leave request management with a four-type leave policy, monthly salary processing with gross and net calculations, and payroll adjustment handling for bonuses and penalties. Extended features include an internal notification system, real-time email delivery for key HR events, scheduled meeting management with auto-generated Google Meet links, company-wide announcement broadcasting, and a profile picture approval workflow with  Cloudinary -based image hosting.   A notable innovation is the integration of  HRMS-AI , an intelligent HR assistant powered by the  Groq  API ( LLaMA  3.3-70B), offering three interaction modes — Normal, Deep Think (with database tool-calling), and Executive (with leave approval capabilities) — enabling natural-language queries against live organizational data in both Arabic and English. The system follows the   Repository and Unit of Work patterns , uses  AutoMapper  for DTO transformations, and exposes a RESTful API with standardized response envelopes, global exception handling middleware, and paginated results. The  frontend , built as a  Progressive Web App (PWA) , features dark mode, RTL/LTR language switching, PDF report generation, and real-time token usage monitoring via  SignalR . K awadir  demonstrates a production-ready, scalable HRMS solution that reduces administrative overhead, improves HR visibility, and introduces AI-assisted decision-making to the modern workplace. From tracking employee attendance and processing monthly payroll to handling leave requests and coordinating internal meetings, HR departments are constantly juggling a high volume of tasks — many of which are still handled through manual processes, spreadsheets, or fragmented tools that do not communicate with one another. This disconnect not only increases the risk of administrative errors but also slows down decision-making and reduces the overall employee experience. As organizations grow, these inefficiencies become harder to ignore. What a small team can manage manually becomes unworkable at scale, and the absence of a centralized, reliable HR system begins to reflect directly on productivity and employee satisfaction.   This project was motivated by that gap.  Kaw a dir  is a full-stack Human Resource Management System built to replace fragmented HR workflows with a single, cohesive platform that covers the complete employee lifecycle. Rather than automating tasks in isolation, the system was designed around how HR teams  actually operate  — where payroll depends on attendance, leave balances affect scheduling, and timely communication is as important as accurate data. What makes  Kawadir  go beyond a conventional HRMS is the addition of an AI assistant integrated directly into the platform. Instead of simply storing and retrieving HR data, the system allows HR personnel to query that data conversationally, reason over it, and in certain cases act on it — all through natural language. This shifts the role of the system from a passive record-keeper to an active operational tool. The following chapters describe the design decisions, technologies, and implementation details behind  Kaw a dir , with the goal of demonstrating that a graduation project can meet the standards of a production-ready enterprise system. Chapter One:  An Overview 2 The Aim  The aim of this project is to design and implement a full-stack Human Resource Management System that enables organizations to manage their complete employee lifecycle through a centralized, secure, and intelligent platform. Kawadir  provides automated workflows for attendance tracking, payroll processing, leave management, and internal communication, while integrating an AI-powered assistant capable of querying and acting on live organizational data through natural language, offering HR teams a modern and efficient solution for reducing administrative overhead and improving data-driven decision-making. Statement of Problem Many organizations still rely on manual procedures, spreadsheets, or fragmented software solutions to manage human resource operations. These traditional approaches often result in inefficient workflows, inconsistent employee records, delayed payroll processing, and difficulties in tracking attendance, leave requests, and employee performance accurately. In addition, the absence of centralized data management limits communication efficiency and reduces the organization’s ability to make informed decisions based on real-time workforce information. Existing HR systems may provide partial solutions; however, many lack integration, scalability, intelligent automation, and real-time interaction capabilities. Organizations frequently face challenges when attempting to manage multiple HR operations across separate platforms, leading to increased administrative overhead and reduced productivity.   Furthermore, most conventional HR systems do not incorporate modern artificial intelligence technologies that can assist HR departments in analyzing employee data, automating repetitive tasks, and improving decision-making processes. This creates a gap between traditional HR management practices and the growing need for smart, data-driven enterprise solutions. Therefore, there is a need for a centralized, secure, scalable, and intelligent Human Resource Management System that integrates core HR functionalities within a single platform while leveraging modern software architecture and AI technologies to improve operational efficiency, employee experience, and organizational decision-making. Chapter One:  An Overview 3 Possible  Solutions To address the limitations of traditional HR management methods, several solutions can be considered. One possible approach is the adoption of a centralized Human Resource Management System (HRMS) that integrates employee management, attendance tracking, payroll processing, leave management, notifications, and communication tools into a unified platform. Such integration reduces administrative complexity, minimizes human error, and improves workflow efficiency. Another possible solution is the implementation of cloud-based and real-time technologies that enable instant communication, live notifications, and remote accessibility. This approach enhances collaboration between employees, HR personnel, and administrators while ensuring that organizational data remains synchronized and accessible across the system. In addition, incorporating artificial intelligence technologies into HR systems can significantly improve decision-making and operational productivity. AI-powered assistants can help automate repetitive HR tasks, analyze employee-related data, provide intelligent insights, and assist users through conversational interactions. This reduces the workload on HR departments and improves the overall user experience.   Based on these possible solutions, the proposed system,  Kaw a dir  HRMS, was developed as a comprehensive enterprise-grade platform that combines centralized HR management, modern software architecture, real-time communication, and AI-powered assistance within a secure and scalable environment. Main Concepts and Technologies The development of  Kawadir  HRMS is based on several modern software engineering concepts and technologies that together provide a scalable, secure, and maintainable enterprise solution. One of the core concepts used in the project is Clean Architecture, which separates the system into independent layers including Domain, Application, Infrastructure, and Presentation layers. This architectural approach improves maintainability, testability, and scalability by reducing dependencies between components and enforcing separation of concerns. The backend of the system was developed using  ASP.NET Core .NET 8 , which provides a powerful and high-performance framework for building RESTful web APIs and enterprise-level applications. The system also utilizes  Entity Framework Core  as an Object-Relational Mapping (ORM) framework to simplify database operations and support code-first database design and migrations. Chapter One:  An Overview 4 For data storage and management,  PostgreSQL  was selected as the primary relational database management system due to its reliability, performance, and strong support for advanced relational features. The frontend was implemented using  Angular 19 , a modern TypeScript-based framework for developing responsive Single Page Applications (SPA). In addition, the frontend supports  Progressive Web Application (PWA)  capabilities, allowing the system to provide  an installable  and responsive user experience across multiple devices. To support secure authentication and authorization, the system uses  JWT (JSON Web Tokens)  for stateless authentication and  role-based access control (RBAC)  to manage permissions for employees, HR staff, and administrators. Passwords are securely stored using  BCrypt  hashing .   The system further incorporates  Artificial Intelligence (AI)  technologies through integration with  Groq  Cloud  and the  Llama 3.3 language model . The AI assistant provides conversational HR support, intelligent analysis, and administrative automation capabilities through multiple operational modes. Additional technologies used in the project include  AutoMapper  for object mapping,  Swagger/ OpenAPI  for API documentation,  Cloudinary  for cloud image storage and profile picture management,  MailKit  for email services, and  Chart.js  for dashboard data visualization. Together, these concepts and technologies form a modern enterprise-grade HR platform capable of handling complex organizational workflows efficiently and securely. The Implemented System   The implemented  Kaw a dir  Human Resource Management System (HRMS) consists of several integrated subsystems that work together to provide a comprehensive and efficient solution for managing human resource operations within an organization. These subsystems are designed to automate administrative processes, improve communication, enhance decision-making, and ensure secure access to organizational data. The main subsystems of the implemented system are: Authentication and Authorization Subsystem:   This subsystem is responsible for managing user authentication and access control. It allows users to securely log in to the system using JWT-based authentication and ensures that each user can access only the features permitted by their assigned role. The subsystem supports password management, session validation, and role-based authorization for Employees, HR Officers, and Administrators. Employee Management Subsystem: The Employee Management subsystem handles all employee-related information within the organization. It enables administrators and HR personnel to create, update, view, and manage employee profiles, including personal information, employment details, department assignments, positions, and employment status. The subsystem also generates unique employee identifiers and maintains employee records throughout their employment lifecycle. Department and Position Management Subsystem :  Manages organizational departments, job positions, salary ranges, and hierarchical structures. Chapter  Three :  The Implemented System, Tests and Empirical Outcomes 20 Leave Management Subsystem :  The Leave Management subsystem automates the process of requesting, reviewing, approving, and rejecting employee leave requests. Employees can submit leave applications, while HR personnel and administrators can review requests and make decisions. The subsystem tracks annual leave balances, prevents overlapping leave requests, records approval history, and maintains complete leave records for all employees. Attendance Management Subsystem: This subsystem records employee attendance through clock-in and clock-out operations. It calculates working hours, maintains attendance history, and automatically handles incomplete attendance sessions when necessary. The subsystem enables both employees and management to monitor attendance performance and supports accurate payroll processing. The  adoption of Clean Architecture principles improved the  maintainability,                        scalability, and modularity of the system, allowing different system components  to remain independent and easier to extend in future development.          3 .  The  implementation of role-based access control, JWT authentication, and  secure password hashing mechanisms enhanced system security and ensured  that users can only access functionalities according to their assigned roles.          4 .  The  integration of real-time technologies such as  SignalR  improved user  experience by providing instant notifications and live system updates without  requiring page refreshes.          5 .  The  AI Assistant subsystem demonstrated the potential of artificial intelligence in  supporting HR operations by providing intelligent responses, employee data  analysis, and automated assistance for authorized users.          6 .  The  use of modern technologies such as ASP.NET Core, Angular, PostgreSQL, and  Cloud-based services contributed to building a reliable, responsive, and  production-ready web application.          7. The testing results showed that the system performs efficiently and  fulfills the  functional requirements specified during the analysis and design phases ,  providing  accurate and reliable HR management services.          8 .  The  Progressive Web Application (PWA) implementation enabled users to access           the system from both desktop and mobile devices, improving accessibility and  flexibility in different working environments. Chapter Four :  Conclusions, Limitations and Future Works 3 9 Limitations  The  researchers  suffers  from many limitations during the design and implement system, as listed in the following:               1. Limited access to real organizational data due to privacy and confidentiality                   concerns. As a result, testing and evaluation were primarily conducted using  simulated data and demonstration scenarios.               2 .  Time  constraints associated with the graduation project schedule limited the  implementation of additional advanced features, such as comprehensive  analytics, multi-organization support, and more sophisticated AI capabilities.               3.  Dependence on external cloud services, including AI and image storage  providers, may affect system availability and performance when internet  connectivity is unstable or when third-party service limitations are encountered.               4 .  The  system was tested within a controlled academic environment and has not  yet been evaluated under large-scale enterprise workloads involving thousands  of concurrent users.               5. Some advanced HR functionalities, such as performance evaluation  management, recruitment workflows, and employee training management, were  considered outside the scope of the current project and may be included in  future versions. Suggestions for Future Works The  designed and implemented system presents many fruitful lines of continued graduation research, and opens the door to a range  if  future work, as listed in the following: Integrating advanced data analytics and business intelligence tools to provide predictive insights regarding employee performance, attendance patterns, workforce planning, and organizational productivity. Expanding the AI Assistant capabilities by incorporating more advanced large language models, voice interaction features, and intelligent decision-support mechanisms for HR managers and administrators. Developing a dedicated mobile application for Android and iOS platforms to complement the existing Progressive Web Application (PWA) and provide  an enhanced  mobile user experience.
